HOW IT WORKS

Understanding Microwave Pyrolysis

Pyrolysis is the process of breaking down materials using heat in the absence of oxygen. Think of it like "cooking" waste materials to transform them into useful products without burning them.

Our technology uses microwave energy (similar to a microwave oven) to heat waste materials rapidly and efficiently. This breaks the chemical bonds in plastics and organic waste, converting them into clean fuel oils.

The process happens in two main stages that work together continuously:

Stage 1: Heating & Decomposition

Waste materials are heated to 500¬∞C using targeted microwave energy. The microwaves (shown as expanding circles) break down the waste into vapor without combustion.

Waste Input

Plastic and organic waste materials are loaded into the sealed pyrolysis reactor chamber. No oxygen is present inside.

02

Microwave Heating

Targeted microwave energy rapidly heats the waste to 500¬∞C, breaking molecular bonds efficiently. This is faster and more precise than traditional heating.

03

Vapor Formation

Materials decompose into vapor and gases without burning (no combustion). The vapor rises and flows into the condensation system.

04

Fuel Collection

Vapors are cooled and condensed into clean, usable fuel oils. This fuel can power vehicles, generators, or industrial equipment.
